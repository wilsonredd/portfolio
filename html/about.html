About

I am currently a computer science student at Brigham Young University. For the past two years, I have worked as a research assistant at the Perception, Cognition, and Control Lab, a deep learning research lab, on BYU's campus.

My research project is focused on applying deep learning to cancer diagnosis. Our goal is to increase the breadth and quality of cancer screenings in rural areas of India. Currently, we are researching self-supervision pretraining techniques. In January 2018, I was able to travel to India to understand how our research would viable function in that environment. I alsp attended NeurIPS 2019 in Vancouver, Canada to deepen my knowledge of current state of the art deep learning research.

I am also currently working on a capstone team in partnership with Adobe. We are creating a solution to enable more efficent monitoring of their internal data streams, such as data center memory and latency. The project is focused on multi-variant time series anomaly detection.

Throughout my undergraduate experience, I have been able to work on a variety of projects ranging from a Ticket to Ride Android app, to multiple deep learning projects, to most recently front-end and back-end web development.

Last summer, I worked as a Software Development Engineer Intern at Amazon Web Services in Seattle, Washington. My projected sped up their account event publishing rate by 90%.

I was the team volunteer manager on the BYU Supermileage vehicle team where we placed 1st against over 100 collegiate teams at the 2018 Shell Eco-marathon Americas at Sonoma Raceway, CA.
